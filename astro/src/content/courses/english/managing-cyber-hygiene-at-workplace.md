In today's digital workplace, cyber hygiene has become as essential as personal hygiene. Organizations face constant threats from sophisticated cyber attacks, data breaches, and insider risks. This intensive one-day course equips professionals with practical knowledge and strategies to establish and maintain effective cyber hygiene practices that protect organizational assets and ensure business continuity.

## Course Overview

Practising the basics of security is the minimum every organization should have. It should be part of the organizational practice to ensure the safety of identity, assets and other details valuable to your organization and the clients you serve are in order.

This course provides comprehensive insights into cyber hygiene fundamentals, practical implementation strategies, and the organizational culture required to maintain robust cybersecurity practices in the workplace.

## Learning Objectives

At the end of the course, participants will be able to understand the constituents of good cyber hygiene for the organization and be able to refer to the relevant principles in its adoption.

- **Cyber Hygiene Constituents**: Identify key components of effective cyber hygiene programs
- **Bad Cyber Hygiene Recognition**: Recognize poor practices and their potential impacts through real examples
- **Cyber Hygiene Principles**: Master fundamental principles for implementing cyber hygiene
- **Practical Application**: Apply learning through exercises and games to real workplace scenarios

## Who Should Attend

This course is designed for:

- IT security professionals and cyber risk managers
- Department heads and business managers
- HR professionals responsible for security awareness
- Compliance and risk management officers
- Employees with access to sensitive data
- Anyone responsible for implementing security policies

## Course Curriculum

### Module 1: Introduction to Cyber Hygiene
- Understanding cyber hygiene in the modern workplace
- Evolution of cybersecurity threats and their workplace impact
- Business consequences of poor cyber hygiene
- Regulatory and compliance requirements

### Module 2: Core Components of Cyber Hygiene
- People, processes, and technology framework
- Physical security and access controls
- Digital asset protection and data handling
- Network security fundamentals

### Module 3: Common Cyber Hygiene Mistakes
- Password management failures
- Phishing and social engineering vulnerabilities
- Unsecured devices and remote work risks
- Data handling and sharing pitfalls

### Module 4: Cyber Hygiene Best Practices
- Password policies and multi-factor authentication
- Safe email and internet usage
- Device security and endpoint protection
- Data classification and handling procedures

### Module 5: Implementing Cyber Hygiene Programs
- Creating organizational cyber hygiene policies
- Employee training and awareness programs
- Regular security assessments and audits
- Incident response and breach notification procedures

### Module 6: Technology Solutions for Cyber Hygiene
- Endpoint protection platforms
- Security information and event management (SIEM)
- Data loss prevention (DLP) tools
- Automated security monitoring

### Module 7: Measuring and Improving Cyber Hygiene
- Key performance indicators for cyber hygiene
- Regular assessment and audit processes
- Continuous improvement methodologies
- Building a security-conscious culture

### Module 8: Interactive Workshop
- Hands-on exercises for cyber hygiene implementation
- Role-playing common security scenarios
- Group discussions on organizational challenges
- Action planning for workplace implementation
