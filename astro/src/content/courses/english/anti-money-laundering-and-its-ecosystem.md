In today's interconnected global financial system, money laundering poses an increasingly sophisticated threat to the integrity of financial institutions worldwide. With incidents rising in both frequency and complexity, organizations must stay ahead of evolving laundering techniques and regulatory requirements. This intensive one-day course provides comprehensive insights into the AML ecosystem, equipping financial professionals with the knowledge and tools to effectively combat financial crime.

## Course Overview

Money laundering and terrorist financing continue to evolve, exploiting new technologies and financial products. Financial institutions face mounting pressure from regulators, increasing compliance costs, and potential reputational damage. This course provides a holistic view of the AML landscape, covering regulatory frameworks, risk assessment methodologies, technology solutions, and practical implementation strategies.

Participants will gain critical insights into how modern technology and IT systems play a crucial role in detecting, preventing, and reporting suspicious activities, while understanding the broader regulatory and operational context.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

- **Understand the AML Ecosystem**: Gain comprehensive knowledge of the money laundering process, typologies, and the global AML framework
- **Technology Perspective**: Understand the IT systems, processes, and technological measures organizations deploy to combat money laundering and terrorist financing
- **Risk Assessment**: Learn to identify red flags and assess money laundering risks across different financial products and customer segments
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Master the key AML regulations including FATF recommendations, local compliance requirements, and reporting obligations
- **Global Challenges**: Understand the implications of offshore financial centers, shell companies, and non-cooperative jurisdictions on money laundering activities
- **Preventive Measures**: Learn about the comprehensive measures financial institutions implement to deter money laundering and terrorist financing
- **Technology Solutions**: Explore modern AML technologies including transaction monitoring systems, KYC platforms, and AI-driven detection tools
- **Practical Application**: Apply learned concepts to real-world scenarios through case studies and interactive discussions

## Who Should Attend

## Course Curriculum

### Session 1: The AML Landscape

**Understanding Money Laundering**
- Definition and stages: Placement, Layering, Integration
- Evolution of money laundering techniques
- Current trends and emerging threats
- Impact on financial institutions and society

**Money Laundering Typologies**
- Trade-based money laundering
- Cash-intensive businesses and shell companies
- Real estate and high-value asset transactions
- Digital currencies and new payment methods
- Exploitation of fintech and digital banking

### Session 2: Regulatory Framework

**Global AML Standards**
- Financial Action Task Force (FATF) 40 Recommendations
- International cooperation and information sharing
- Country risk ratings and implications
- High-risk and non-cooperative jurisdictions

**Local Regulatory Requirements**
- Relevant AML/CFT regulations and guidelines
- Regulatory reporting requirements
- Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)
- Cash Transaction Reporting (CTR)
- Penalties and enforcement actions

### Session 3: AML Technology and Systems

**IT Infrastructure for AML**
- Transaction monitoring systems architecture
- Customer Due Diligence (CDD) platforms
- Know Your Customer (KYC) technology
- Sanctions screening systems
- Case management and investigation tools

**Advanced Technologies**
- Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning in AML
- Data analytics for pattern detection
- Blockchain analytics for cryptocurrency monitoring
- RegTech solutions and automation
- API integrations and real-time monitoring

### Session 4: Risk Management and Controls

**AML Risk Assessment**
- Enterprise-wide risk assessment methodology
- Customer risk rating models
- Product and service risk evaluation
- Geographic and jurisdictional risks
- Risk mitigation strategies

**Preventive Measures and Controls**
- Customer identification and verification programs
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers
- Ongoing monitoring and periodic reviews
- Employee training and awareness programs
- Independent testing and audit requirements

### Session 5: Practical Implementation

**Red Flags and Detection**
- Common suspicious activity indicators
- Transaction patterns suggesting money laundering
- Customer behavior red flags
- Internal alert escalation procedures

**Case Studies and Real-World Examples**
- Analysis of major money laundering cases
- Regulatory enforcement actions and lessons learned
- Best practices from leading financial institutions
- Emerging challenges in cryptocurrency and DeFi

**Offshore Financial Centers**
- Role in the global financial system
- Challenges in transparency and information exchange
- Due diligence for offshore entities
- Beneficial ownership identification

### Session 6: Building an Effective AML Program

**Program Components**
- Governance structure and senior management oversight
- AML policies and procedures development
- Technology infrastructure and system requirements
- Testing and independent review
- Continuous improvement and adaptation

**Emerging Challenges**
- Digital assets and decentralized finance (DeFi)
- Cross-border e-commerce and payment platforms
- Privacy coins and anonymity-enhanced cryptocurrencies
- Regulatory technology (RegTech) opportunities
