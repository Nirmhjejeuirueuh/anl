As organizations accelerate their digital transformation journeys, the cybersecurity threat landscape has expanded exponentially. Emerging technologies like Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, IoT, Blockchain, and Cloud Computing offer tremendous opportunities but also introduce new and complex cyber risks. This comprehensive 2-day course equips financial professionals with the knowledge and tools to understand, assess, and manage these evolving cyber threats in an increasingly digital world.

**Secure Today, Agile Tomorrow** - we help you build resilient cybersecurity frameworks that protect your business while enabling digital transformation. Contact us at enquiry@agilenlite.com to discuss your cybersecurity training needs.

## Course Overview

This intensive module provides a deep dive into the cybersecurity landscape across various countries and key industries, with particular focus on the financial services sector. Through a combination of theoretical frameworks, practical case studies, and hands-on risk assessment exercises, participants will develop a comprehensive understanding of cyber threats, vulnerabilities, and the controls needed to manage them effectively.

The course addresses the critical intersection of emerging technologies and cybersecurity, enabling participants to make informed decisions about technology adoption while maintaining robust security postures.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

- **Analyze Emerging Technology Risks**: Evaluate the incremental cyber risks and vulnerabilities arising from AI, Machine Learning, IoT, Blockchain, and Cloud technologies
- **Global Threat Landscape**: Understand the cybersecurity landscape across different countries and industries, including region-specific threats and regulatory requirements
- **Risk Assessment Proficiency**: Apply systematic risk assessment techniques to identify, analyze, and prioritize cyber risks in financial organizations
- **Controls Framework**: Describe and implement key internal and external controls for managing threats and vulnerabilities effectively
- **Best Practices Implementation**: Understand and apply cybersecurity best practices through real-world case studies from leading organizations
- **Employee Security**: Develop strategies for employee protection, security awareness, and education programs
- **Security Technologies**: Evaluate and recommend appropriate security technologies including biometrics, encryption, and access controls
- **Incident Response**: Understand incident response frameworks and develop practical response plans
- **Compliance and Governance**: Navigate regulatory requirements and establish effective cybersecurity governance structures
- **Strategic Planning**: Develop cybersecurity strategies that align with business objectives and risk appetite

## Course Curriculum

### Day 1: Understanding the Cyber Risk Landscape

**Module 1: Global Cybersecurity Landscape**
- Current state of cybersecurity threats worldwide
- Industry-specific threats in financial services
- Threat actors: Nation-states, cybercriminals, hacktivists, insiders
- Recent major cyber incidents and lessons learned
- Regional differences in cyber threats and regulations
- Cost of cyber incidents: Financial and reputational impact

**Module 2: Emerging Technology Risks**

*Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning*
- AI-powered cyber attacks and defense
- Data poisoning and model manipulation
- Privacy concerns and ethical considerations
- Adversarial machine learning

*Internet of Things (IoT)*
- IoT device vulnerabilities and attack vectors
- Securing IoT ecosystems in financial environments
- Network segmentation and isolation strategies
- Supply chain security for IoT devices

*Blockchain and Distributed Ledger Technology*
- Smart contract vulnerabilities
- Consensus mechanism attacks (51% attacks)
- Private key management and custody solutions
- Cryptocurrency exchange security

*Cloud Computing*
- Shared responsibility model in cloud security
- Cloud-specific threats: Misconfigurations, data breaches
- Multi-cloud and hybrid cloud security challenges
- Cloud access security broker (CASB) solutions

**Module 3: Cyber Threat Taxonomy**
- Malware: Ransomware, trojans, worms, viruses
- Phishing and social engineering attacks
- Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)
- Zero-day exploits and vulnerabilities
- Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks
- Supply chain attacks
- Insider threats

**Module 4: Risk Assessment Frameworks**
- Introduction to cyber risk assessment methodologies
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- ISO 27001 risk assessment process
- FAIR (Factor Analysis of Information Risk)
- Qualitative vs. quantitative risk assessment
- Hands-on exercise: Conducting a basic cyber risk assessment

### Day 2: Managing and Mitigating Cyber Risks

**Module 5: Controls Framework**

*Technical Controls*
- Network security: Firewalls, IDS/IPS, network segmentation
- Endpoint protection and EDR solutions
- Identity and access management (IAM)
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Data loss prevention (DLP)
- Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)
- Encryption technologies and key management

*Administrative Controls*
- Security policies and procedures
- Risk management frameworks
- Security governance structures
- Third-party risk management
- Vendor security assessments
- Security awareness training programs

*Physical Controls*
- Data center security
- Physical access controls
- Environmental controls
- Biometric security systems

**Module 6: Security Technologies Deep Dive**

*Biometric Security*
- Types of biometric authentication
- Implementation considerations and best practices
- Privacy and regulatory implications
- Spoofing and liveness detection

*Advanced Security Technologies*
- Zero Trust Architecture
- Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response (SOAR)
- Threat intelligence platforms
- Deception technologies (honeypots, honeynets)
- Blockchain for cybersecurity applications

**Module 7: Case Studies and Best Practices**

*Major Cyber Incident Analysis*
- Financial sector breaches: Root causes and aftermath
- Ransomware attacks on critical infrastructure
- Insider threat cases
- Supply chain compromises

*Best Practice Implementation*
- Lessons from leading organizations
- Building a security-first culture
- Continuous monitoring and improvement
- Security metrics and KPIs

**Module 8: Employee Protection and Education**
- Human factor in cybersecurity
- Designing effective security awareness programs
- Phishing simulation and training
- Secure remote work practices
- BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) policies
- Insider threat mitigation strategies

**Module 9: Incident Response and Business Continuity**
- Incident response lifecycle: Prepare, Detect, Contain, Eradicate, Recover
- Building an incident response team and playbooks
- Crisis communication and stakeholder management
- Cyber insurance considerations
- Business continuity and disaster recovery planning
- Regulatory reporting requirements

**Module 10: Cybersecurity Governance and Compliance**
- Board-level cybersecurity oversight
- Regulatory landscape: GDPR, MAS TRM, PDPA, etc.
- Cybersecurity audit and compliance requirements
- Third-party and supply chain risk management
- Cybersecurity strategy development
- Budget allocation and ROI for security investments

**Module 11: Future Trends and Emerging Challenges**
- Quantum computing threats to cryptography
- 5G security implications
- AI-driven security operations
- Evolution of regulatory requirements
- Building resilient security architectures
